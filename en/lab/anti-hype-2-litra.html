“2 liters of water” is marketing, not physiology

A careful biohacker asks first: is this data — or a catchy slogan?

A careful biohacker asks first: is this data — or a catchy slogan?

“I force 2 liters a day and feel like an aquarium.” Short answer: 2L is marketing more often than physiology.

Same pattern as a dozen “must-do” biohacks: hard numbers with no body weight, climate, activity, or kidney context. Careful doesn’t mean “drink less.” It means “don’t buy dogma.” Sleep, light, movement, food, stress beat a stack of rituals.

Hype filter

Run any feed tip through three questions: (1) Study or mechanism? (2) Personal context? (3) What if I skip it for a week? If you don’t know — it’s content, not a protocol.
