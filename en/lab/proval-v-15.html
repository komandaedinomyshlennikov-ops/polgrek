The 3 p.m. crash isn’t laziness

Clear morning, fog after lunch? More often multi-system physiology than “pull yourself together.”

Clear morning, fog after lunch? More often multi-system physiology than “pull yourself together.”

Energy isn’t a moral resource. At minimum: fuel and recovery, attention load, and circadian timing (light, clock, sleep). Fix only one — and wonder why “doing everything right” fails.

The post-lunch crash often mixes heavy food, office light, long focus without breaks, and stockpiled stress. The brain isn’t “breaking from laziness.” It’s conserving.

Short audit

Honest check: sleep last 3 nights, movement last 2 days, morning light, screen-free pause after 1 p.m. If answers are “meh,” you don’t need a secret protocol. You need base. Anatomy of Energy is about not fixing one system while feeding hype.

N-of-1 for 5 days

One lever: 10–15 minutes walk in daylight after lunch. Leave the rest alone. Rate fog 1–10. If it shifts — that’s data, not “I finally got disciplined.”
