The brain loves control — especially when it has none

Panic under uncertainty isn’t weakness. It’s a system without a clear next step.

Panic under uncertainty isn’t weakness. It’s a system without a clear next step.

The brain loves the feeling of control. Even when it doesn’t have any. Especially then.

That’s why uncertainty panic isn’t a character flaw. Give the system one concrete next step and the noise drops.

Energy spent fighting reality

Most people underestimate how much fuel goes into arguing with what is. Fighting “how it should be” often costs more than dealing with “how it is.”

One next step

Write the thing hanging over you. Cross out everything except one action in the next 24 hours — small enough that skipping it would be embarrassing. Do it. Panic often falls from one finished step, not a five-year plan.
