Minimum effective dose isn’t laziness — it’s a filter

If a protocol needs a pharmacy cabinet to “work,” it probably doesn’t work for long.

If a protocol needs a pharmacy cabinet to “work,” it probably doesn’t work for long.

Minimum effective dose isn’t laziness. It’s the opposite of a “just in case” stack. More protocols often mean more noise, less sleep, more anxiety that you’re “missing something.”

A careful biohacker asks: mechanism? context? what if I skip a week? Base (sleep, light, movement, food, stress) beats “2 liters and 12 miracle steps.”

How not to break your mind with “optimization”

One lever → measurable effect → next. Not ten in parallel. Brain Biohacking covers why fast methods burn out and where nootropics stop being smart.

One lever for 7 days

One only: sleep window ±30 min steadier, or 20 minutes walk, or screens off 30 min before bed. Leave the rest. End of week: what actually moved? Keep only that.
