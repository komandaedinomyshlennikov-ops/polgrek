Minimum effective dose isn’t laziness — it’s a filter

If a protocol needs a pharmacy cabinet to “work,” it probably doesn’t work for long.

If a protocol needs a pharmacy cabinet to “work,” it probably doesn’t work for long.

Minimum effective dose (MED) isn’t laziness. It’s the opposite of a “just in case” stack. More protocols often mean more noise, less sleep, more FOMO that you’re missing something.

A careful biohacker asks: mechanism? context? cost of skipping a week? Base (sleep, light, movement, food, stress) beats the “2 liters + 12 pills” ritual.

Don’t break your mind “optimizing”

One lever, measurable effect, then the next. Not ten in parallel. Brain Biohacking covers why fast methods burn out and where nootropics stop being smart.

One lever, 7 days

Pick one: stabler sleep ±30 min, or 20 minutes walking, or screens off 30 min before bed. Touch nothing else. End of week: what actually moved? Keep only that.
