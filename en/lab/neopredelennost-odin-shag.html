Uncertainty isn’t fixed by a yearly plan — by one step

The brain loves control — especially when it has none. Panic is often a request for a clear task, not a weak character.

The brain loves control — especially when it has none. Panic is often a request for a clear task, not a weak character.

Uncertainty eats resource: scenarios spin, cortisol holds the background, decisions go tunnel-vision. A huge yearly plan sometimes adds noise. A small finished step reduces it.

Give the system one task for 24 hours — small enough that it’s embarrassing not to do it. Panic often eases not from “full future clarity,” but from the signal: “I already moved.” Not weakness. A control request you can dose.

One card for the day

On paper: “today I will ___.” No second line. Evening: checkmark or move with a one-sentence reason. Not a report to a boss. A signal to the brain.
