Brain at 100+ A science-backed plan to keep your mind sharp for decades

Most people assume the brain will “just hold on” until retirement. Brains don’t work that way. Cognitive longevity isn’t a lottery ticket or a genetic sentence. It’s the sum of daily choices that either build reserve — or slowly burn it down.

This book isn’t about becoming a genius in a week. It’s about not losing clarity while you’re busy with “more important” things.

Most longevity talk sells miracle fixes. The boring levers win: sleep architecture, movement that raises heart rate, blood pressure, blood sugar, and hard thinking that builds reserve. Skip the genius-in-a-week pitch. Protect clarity while you run a real life.
